Engineering interpretation

  • Wet expansion turbines materially improve liquid yield and reduce exergy destruction relative to simple JT throttling.
  • Subcooling with 2 K return vapor strongly suppresses flash-gas generation and stabilizes downstream inventory.
  • The QLH buffer should be interpreted as a thermodynamic shock absorber, not a competing architecture.
  • Equivalent 2 K heat leak directly consumes cryogenic margin and should become a tracked operational KPI.
